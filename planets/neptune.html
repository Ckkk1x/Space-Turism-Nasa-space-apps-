NEPTUNE

THE PLANET OF EXTREME EXPERIENCES

Windiest planet
Largely made up of water, ammonia, and methane in solid form
Triton satelite, with a ocean under the iced surface

Being the furthest planet in our solar system, it makes it perfect to blow off some steam.

Due to its non-solid body, different parts of Neptune rotate at different speeds and therefore, strong winds are attracted to this planet as well as some crazy people ready to conquer their force.

As the atmosphere in Neptune is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, the hydrogen powered jet engines of the zeppelins are the perfect way to enjoy the storm's view but from a secure distance and comfortable temperature (-200ºC is the average temperature in the zone!).

There are 14 satellites around Neptune, but there is one that stands out: Triton. It is the biggest satellite of Neptune, but the strangest thing is that it is the only big satellite in our solar system that rotates in the opposite direction to its planet. It has an iced surface, but the ocean under the surface offers an incredible diving option.
